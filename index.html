List of European Countries with the Most Work Opportunities in 2023

Could you quantify the excitement of going to a foreign country for work or study? The answer to this question is simple. You cannot. Imagine the level of excitement for going to a region where you can travel freely across 27 countries without the complex requirements of a visa and other necessary documents. About 8.84 million non-EU citizens were employed in the labour market of Europe in 2021 to earn a living in different European countries, with many who send money online to their families for financial support. Some even send money to Ghana from Australia for their loved ones.

This blog will shed light on the reasons which attract people to Europe with the list of top European countries with the most work opportunities for job-seeking expatriates.

Why do people from outside Europe find travelling to the EU attractive?

Reasons abound. Besides, you may have different reasons to travel to foreign countries, which may not resonate well with other travellers. But as mentioned above, the biggest advantage of travelling to the EU is that you can travel to several countries freely. This, in turn, can offer several opportunities to you both for work and education and brighten your prospects of earning a better income than you can in your native country. Most of the expatriates, as you know, hail from low- to middle-income countries (LMICs) where finding a progressive career, job, or quality education has always remained a huge challenge.

Multiple travelling and work opportunities

The European Union consists of 27 countries. So, going to Europe is not like travelling to one country where you will have access to a limited number of opportunities. It is rather going to a bunch of countries with as diverse and several opportunities as the number of countries forming the EU bunch!

Remember that each member state's number of opportunities runs into hundreds of thousands.

The robust and accommodating economy of the EU

That the EU consists of several countries gives it leverage over other Western countries with equally robust economies. Due to this culmination of different economies, the EU accommodates job-seekers in almost all professional fields.

In the post-Covid-19 era, in 2019, about 1.1% of the employed people, on average, in the EU worked in the tourism sector. It accounted for over 7.4 million jobs in this sector alone. Imagine the potential across the entire employment sector!

The numbers dipped due to the pandemic but are sure to rise again.

A look at the fast facts of the European economy

  • The European Union is a single market comprising 27 countries.
  • In 2021, the EU's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) was €14.5 trillion.
  • The EU accounts for about 14% of the global trade in goods.
  • According to reports by the World Bank, GDP per capita income in the EU was $38,234.1 in 2021.
  • The employment rate in the EU was 59.1% in 2021 for non-EU citizens.

These statistics point to the potential the EU has for expatriates seeking jobs there to earn a living and support their families through money transfers.

Let’s now enlist the top European countries for a wide range of job opportunities.

The United Kingdom

There are around a million jobs available in the UK, with teachers, veterinary nurses, hospitality professionals, and healthcare professionals predicted to take up a major chunk of the vacated positions.

The Netherlands

Although significantly smaller than the UK, the Netherlands still offers abundant jobs, primarily in sectors such as agriculture, customer service, technology, etc.

Belgium

With jobs available in cyber security, marketing, human resources, etc., about 200,000 jobs are currently available in Belgium.

France

France is the most popular European country to visit for any purpose. With about a million jobs available in the country, a huge number of opportunities are available in social care, retail and manufacturing, healthcare, and the construction industry, which is opening different positions within the industry.

Spain

With around 150,000 jobs available in manufacturing, telemarketing, and accounting fields, Spain hosts huge finance companies like Santander, etc. The potential to find jobs in Spain in the abovementioned sectors is immense.

Switzerland

Switzerland offers several jobs in the engineering, legal, and healthcare sectors. Switzerland offers some of the highest salaries across the EU and has over 200,000 jobs available in the country currently.

Italy

You can find thousands of jobs in business, administration, construction and teaching in Italy. Immense potential lies for professionals in computing, mathematics, and sales.

Greece

Several jobs are available in Greece in marketing, IT, and engineering. You can find jobs in these sectors with the country in economic stress notwithstanding.

Sweden

Sweden encourages skilled labour and experts in the financial sector. You can find several jobs in the construction, architecture, legal, accountancy, and civil engineering sectors.

Ukraine

In Ukraine, you can find several job opportunities in the construction, marketing, cooking, and sales professions.

Poland

Ample jobs are available in the country in the agriculture, tourism, manufacturing, IT, and engineering sectors. Besides, the country encourages manual labour such as drivers, etc.

You can find several jobs across all professional fields in the EU and even in labour, with the above-mentioned countries offering the most opportunities.
